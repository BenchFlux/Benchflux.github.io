Advancing a Standard for Carbon Flux Data Across Scales

BenchFlux is a research initiative with the goal of creating a new machine learning benchmark for carbon sequestration data.

BenchFlux is a consortium of research teams working on methods to integrate atmospheric data and remote sensing data across scales. Our goal is to improve on the current paradigm of scale-agnostic carbon flux data by creating a new machine learning benchmark that can consistently marry top-down remote sensing data with bottom-up eddy covariance data. Society faces the urgent challenge of developing sustainable, equitable, and scalable solutions to climate change, driven by the critical need for evidence-based management of life support systems like carbon, water, and heat.

To address this need, we are developing scale-aware benchmarks for measuring land carbon sequestration by leveraging atmospheric carbon flux data alongside in-situ and remotely sensed earth observations.

We aim to provide code tutorials and datasets to the climate community to help scientists and developers add scale awareness into their climate solutions.
